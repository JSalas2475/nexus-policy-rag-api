# Data Retention and Privacy Policy

## Purpose

This policy defines how Nexus Technologies collects, stores, processes, and deletes personal and business data in compliance with applicable privacy laws including GDPR, CCPA, and industry regulations.

## Data Retention Periods

| Data Type | Retention Period | Legal Basis |
|-----------|------------------|-------------|
| Employee records | 7 years after termination | Legal obligation |
| Customer contracts | 7 years after expiration | Legal obligation |
| Financial records | 7 years | Tax regulations |
| Email communications | 3 years | Business interest |
| Application logs | 1 year | Security |
| Customer support tickets | 2 years after resolution | Business interest |
| Marketing consent records | 5 years after withdrawal | GDPR compliance |

## Data Minimization

Nexus Technologies collects only data necessary for specified business purposes. Employees must not store personal data on local devices, personal cloud accounts, or unauthorized systems.

## GDPR Compliance

For EU/EEA data subjects, Nexus Technologies ensures:
- Lawful basis documented for all processing activities
- Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) with all third-party processors
- Right to access requests fulfilled within **30 days**
- Right to erasure requests fulfilled within **30 days** unless legal retention applies
- Data breach notification to supervisory authority within **72 hours**

## CCPA Compliance

California residents have the right to:
- Know what personal information is collected
- Request deletion of personal information
- Opt out of sale of personal information (Nexus Technologies does not sell personal data)
- Non-discrimination for exercising privacy rights

CCPA requests must be fulfilled within **45 days**.

## Data Classification and Handling

Refer to the Information Security Policy for classification levels. Restricted data (SSNs, payment cards) must be encrypted with AES-256 and access logged.

## Data Deletion Procedures

When retention periods expire, data is securely deleted using NIST 800-88 guidelines. IT Operations executes automated deletion jobs monthly. Deletion certificates are maintained for audit purposes.

## Third-Party Processors

All third-party data processors must pass security assessment before onboarding. Processors are reviewed annually. Current approved processors are listed in the Vendor Management Portal.

## Employee Responsibilities

Employees who handle personal data must complete Privacy Training annually. Report data privacy concerns to privacy@nexustech.com within 24 hours.

## Data Subject Requests

Data subject access requests (DSARs) are managed by the Privacy Team. Employees who receive DSARs directly must forward to privacy@nexustech.com within **1 business day**.

## Cross-Border Transfers

Personal data transfers outside the EU/EEA use Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or adequacy decisions. Transfers to non-approved countries require Legal and Privacy Team approval.

## Policy Review

This policy is reviewed annually by Legal and Privacy teams. Last updated: January 2025.
