NEXUS TECHNOLOGIES - INCIDENT REPORTING POLICY

OVERVIEW
All employees must promptly report workplace incidents, safety hazards, security events, and policy violations. Timely reporting protects employees, customers, and the company.

WHAT TO REPORT
- Workplace injuries or near-misses
- Security incidents (data breaches, unauthorized access, lost devices)
- Safety hazards (fire, chemical spills, structural issues)
- Harassment or discrimination
- Fraud or financial misconduct
- Equipment failures affecting business operations

REPORTING TIMELINES
- Injuries: Immediately, and within 1 hour to your manager and Safety Team
- Security incidents: Within 1 hour to security@nexustech.com or 1-800-NEXUS-SOC
- Safety hazards: Within 4 hours to facilities@nexustech.com
- HR incidents: Within 24 hours to HR or the Ethics Hotline (1-888-NEXUS-ETHICS)
- All other incidents: Within 24 hours to your manager

REPORTING METHODS
1. Emergency: Call 911 first for life-threatening situations, then notify Security
2. Security incidents: security@nexustech.com or SOC hotline
3. HR incidents: HR portal or Ethics Hotline (anonymous option available)
4. Safety incidents: facilities@nexustech.com or ext. 9911
5. General incidents: Incident Report Form on the employee portal

INVESTIGATION PROCESS
All reported incidents are logged in the Incident Management System within 2 business hours. Investigations begin within 1 business day for high-severity incidents and within 3 business days for standard incidents. Reporters receive status updates every 5 business days.

NON-RETALIATION
Nexus Technologies prohibits retaliation against employees who report incidents in good faith. Retaliation should be reported immediately to HR or the Ethics Hotline.

DOCUMENTATION
Incident reports must include: date/time, location, people involved, description of event, immediate actions taken, and witnesses. Photos and screenshots may be attached when relevant.

CONTACT
General questions: compliance@nexustech.com
